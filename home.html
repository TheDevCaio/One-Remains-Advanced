One Remains Advanced
  • 1: The game is played on a board with holes arranged in a cross shape.

  • 2: Initially, all the holes on the board are filled with pegs, except for the central hole.

  • 3: The objective of the game is to eliminate as many pegs as possible by jumping over them with other pegs.

  • 4: A peg can jump over another peg horizontally or vertically to an empty hole, and the jumped peg is removed from the board.

  • 5: Only one peg must remain on the board, ideally in the central hole, for the player to win the game.

  • 6: A valid move is when a peg jumps over another peg into an empty hole in a straight line (horizontally or vertically).

  • 7: Diagonal moves are not allowed in this version of the game.

  • 8: The game is considered successful if only one peg remains on the board in the end.

  • 9: If you are stuck and cannot make any more valid moves, the game is over, and you have to restart.

  • 10: The fewer pegs left on the board at the end, the better your performance in the game.
