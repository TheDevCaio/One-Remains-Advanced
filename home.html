One Remains Advanced
  • 1: A match is played between the player and the computer.

  • 2: Each match consists of exactly 6 rounds, ending immediately after the resolution of the 6th round.

  • 3: The objective of the game is to control the majority of the 3 available locations.

  • 4: The player controls a location if the sum of the strengths of their characters at the location is greater than the sum of the opponent's characters also at the location.

  • 5: Each player starts with a deck containing the same 12 character cards. Each card has a name, an energy cost, and a strength value.

  • 6: At the beginning of the match, players randomly draw 4 cards into their hands.

  • 7: At the start of each round, each player receives:
    - A newly drawn card added to their hand;
    - Total energy equal to the current round number (from 1 to 6).

  • 8: The player can spend energy to place the cards from their hand on a valid location.

  • 9: The player cannot place a card if they do not have enough energy to play it.

  • 10: The player cannot place more than 4 cards at a location.

  • 11: Unused energy is lost at the end of the round.

  • 12: The winner will be the one who, at the end of the match, controls the majority of the 3 locations in dispute.
